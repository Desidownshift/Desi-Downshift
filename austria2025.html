Austrian GP 2025: McLaren’s Kinky Dominance Left No One Overtaken (& Most of Us Sleeping)


© 2025 F1 Media. All rights reserved.

Location: Spielberg, Austria
Result: McLaren 1–2, Leclerc sighing in Italian, Hamilton’s Ferrari in P4, overtakes as rare as a sober podium celebration.

Free Practice: Anticipation Without Action

You know the kind of foreplay where everyone talks about getting wild but ends up just checking their phones? That was Friday and Saturday at the Red Bull Ring. FPs were so low on drama, the most action was tire blankets being flirtatiously removed. Drivers practiced long runs studiously — not a single wild slide, no heroic saves, just “data gathering” so dry you’d wish for a sudden sprinkler malfunction. Half the grid looked ready for a nap; the only rising tension was everyone’s caffeine intake.

Sky Sports F1 2025

Qualifying: A Quick Tease, Then DRS (Disappointing Racing Session)

Lando Norris slung his McLaren onto pole with a 1:03.971, just ahead of Leclerc’s Ferrari and Oscar Piastri. Lewis Hamilton was there as always—in fourth, smooth, unphased, and probably already planning his post-race fashion shoot. We almost got a spark when Hamilton’s titanium plank sent up a shower (and some grass burning) in Q2, but even that sizzle fizzled too quickly. Everyone else? Just queued up their runs, slapping in purples like safe words at a party that never gets going.

© 2025 Motorsport Images

The Race: Sixty-Two Laps of Mutual Respect (aka, Yawns & Yearning)

There was a brief flash of drama: rookie Andrea Kimi Antonelli got a little too frisky off the start, brake-checking Max Verstappen and popping him right into early retirement, which meant the home crowd had to go look for the nearest therapist (or bartender). After that? DRS trains that would make a Delhi Metro blush, and overtakes so rare the TV director missed half of them because they were ordering more coffee. Norris and Piastri swapped leads in the pits and with DRS, but it all felt a bit… planned. In the midfield, drivers followed each other for so long it started looking like a travelling circus act—where nobody wanted the solo.

The Race 2025

Leclerc & Hamilton: Stuck in the Friendzone (again)

Charles Leclerc tried everything: undercuts, late braking, positive affirmations… but third was as good as it got. He finished a gloomy P3, another step on a podium that’s starting to feel more like a therapist’s couch.
Lewis Hamilton, meanwhile, was the ultimate wingman: cool, collected, giving no quarter and taking none in return. He brought his Ferrari home a very proper P4, making no mistakes, not threatening Leclerc, and not even featuring in the few “overtakes” shown to the bored audience. He’s the F1 equivalent of a chaperone at an underwhelming party: essential, unflappable, but hardly the one you remember in the morning.

Retirements: Verstappen (first-lap kink crash, not the fun kind), Antonelli (suffering consequences of youthful excitement), Sainz (didn’t even start, mechanical mood killer).

Verdict: Not Even a Whip-Crack

If you like your GPs with kink, drama, and a bit of slipstream passion, Austria 2025 just offered a (very safe) cuddle under a heavy blanket. McLaren got their first 1-2 at the Red Bull Ring in forever, Norris snatched a win, and the rest? Well, let’s just turn the lights off quietly and hope the next Grand Prix brings a little more action—because this one was vanilla with extra marshmallow.
