The Poetry Garage

They say mechanical engineers don't write poems—but I downshifted through emotions the same way I do corners: late, hard, and without warning.
Welcome to the part of Desi Downshift where the torque drops, the throttle rests, and my brain switches from CAD to chaos. This is where the metal meets the metaphor. Where race lines turn into heartbreak curves, and bolt torque becomes the pressure in my chest. Every poem here has been written: On the edge of a breakdown (emotional or thermal), Between simulations that refused to converge, Or while staring at the ceiling wondering why life doesn't come with an undo button. These aren't your grandmother's sonnets. They're raw. Sometimes rough. A little unfiltered, a little unhinged — just like the loose bolt that keeps my life interesting.

I write about:
Love that redlined and blew the gasket. Friendships that coasted in neutral until the fuel ran out. Nights where sleep was optional, but overthinking was not. And moments when even speed couldn't outrun the silence. So if you're here for gears and grease, that's great. But if you're here to feel something?
Welcome to the softest corner of the garage.
