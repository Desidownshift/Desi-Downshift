🇧🇪 Belgian GP 2025: FIA's Famous Snooze, Piastri's Win Party & Hamilton's Setup Headache


© 2025 Formula1.com. All rights reserved.

Location: Spa-Francorchamps, Belgium
Result: McLaren 1–2, Ferrari struggling with battery tech, Red Bull playing catch-up.

The FIA's big move this weekend? Starting the race nearly 80 minutes late—because apparently, watching rain puddles is the new sport etiquette. Visibility was so bad behind the safety car, race control decided it was bedtime rather than go-time. George Russell might have called it sensible, but let's be honest, most fans were wondering if Santa was coming instead of a green flag.

McLAREN Racing ©2025

Oscar Piastri & Lando Norris: McLaren's Dynamic Duo

Once the race finally kicked off, Oscar Piastri wasted no time. The McLaren rookie glued himself to the front and took his sixth win with almost casual confidence. Lando Norris, meanwhile, led the loyal second-place charge, making the team's perfect weekend at Spa a reality.

Charles Leclerc's Slick Setup & Max's Close Pursuit

Charlie was the crafty one with a low-downforce setup, perfect for Spa's long straights. That made him faster on the straights, but also a bit more vulnerable in the twisty bits. Max Verstappen, on the other hand, was loaded with higher downforce to tackle the corners better, but that meant he struggled to make up ground on those faster stretches.

Charles had to work hard to keep Max behind him; the Dutchman pressured like a wolf stalking prey but couldn't quite make a clean pass. Charles played the defensive big brother perfectly—blocking, weaving, and protecting his heels.

© Planet Sport Limited 2025

Hamilton's Epic Climb... Stuck Behind Albon

Lewis Hamilton started that day last on the grid (because, why not spice things up?). He blazed through the field up to P7, but then found himself stuck behind Alex Albon. The problem? Hamilton's car was juiced with more downforce to handle the corners, but that made him slower on Spa's long straights. Add low fuel (meaning he had to play the infamous "lift and coast" game to save tires and fuel), and overtaking was as tricky as juggling chainsaws blindfolded.

Ferrari’s “Li-Co tech” isn’t some flashy new battery upgrade—it’s just their fancy name for the old-school “lift and coast” technique. During Spa 2025, radio messages revealed the team’s constant calls for Charles to lift off early and coast into braking zones to manage fuel and keep the hybrid systems happy. While this cautious approach saved hardware, it absolutely choked the usual Ferrari aggressiveness, forcing them to settle for damage limitation as rivals like McLaren surged ahead. It’s clear Ferrari needs to fix this dependency fast if they want to get back to fighting at the front rather than quietly limping through races.

Other Notable Mentions

Fernando Alonso was showing no signs of slowing down, mixing wisdom with bold moves. Up-and-comer Gabriel Bortoleto snagged a solid P9, proving rookies can bring the heat. And Kimi Antonelli picked up the DHL fastest lap in mixed weather, masters of tricky conditions.



🏁 Final Classification

1. Oscar Piastri — Cool as the Ardennes mist
2. Lando Norris — The perfect wingman
3. Charles Leclerc — Strategic master with defense masterclass
4. Max Verstappen — Relentless but setup-limited
5. George Russell — Sensible in the rain, speedy in the dry
6. Albon — Steady but battery-hampered
7. Lewis Hamilton — Epic climb, frustrating finish
9. Gabriel Bortoleto — Rookie making waves

Verdict:

So there you have it. The 2025 Belgian GP was part strategy, part weather lottery, part setup chess—an epic mix underscored by FIA's cautionary late start and some seriously dance-floor-worthy racing moves once the show finally got going.

Next up: Hungary. Temple of speed, here we come!
